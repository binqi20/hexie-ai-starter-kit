# HeXie AI Starter Toolkit

This repository is a practical starter kit for postgraduate students who want to use AI responsibly in study, research, and early-stage professional work.

It was created for the **HeXie Management Student Inspiring Lab**:

**Coupling Human and Digital Intelligence: A HeXie Approach to AI in Study and Work**

## Core idea

AI is most useful when students couple:

- **Human judgment**: goals, ethics, interpretation, final claims, accountability.
- **Digital systems**: search, summarization, comparison, drafting, analysis, formatting, and repeatable workflow steps.

Do not use this toolkit to outsource your thinking. Use it to make your thinking more structured, evidence-aware, and verifiable.

## Start here

1. Open `START_HERE.md`.
2. Pick one workflow from `toolkit/`.
3. Copy one prompt from `prompts/`.
4. Use the relevant checklist from `templates/`.
5. Verify every important claim against the original source.

## Academic integrity

Follow your module, programme, and university rules. When in doubt, ask your instructor what is allowed.

Safe uses usually include brainstorming, explanation, outline feedback, language improvement, source comparison, and data checking. Risky uses include submitting AI-generated work as your own, inventing citations, hiding AI use when disclosure is required, or uploading sensitive data to personal AI accounts.

## Data safety

Do not upload:

- Personal identifiers.
- Employee records.
- Patient or health data.
- Confidential company data.
- Unpublished interview transcripts without permission.
- Any dataset you are not authorized to share with the AI provider.

Use synthetic, public, or properly de-identified data for practice.

## The default verification habit

Before you trust an AI output, ask:

1. What source supports this claim?
2. Did I open the original source?
3. Is the citation real and relevant?
4. Are the assumptions stated?
5. What would change my conclusion?
