# Workflow: Evidence-Backed Writing

Use this workflow when you already have sources and notes, and you want AI to help with writing without losing academic integrity.

## Rule

Do not start with "write my essay." Start with claims and evidence.

## Inputs

- Research question.
- Claim-evidence matrix.
- Research notes.
- Required format or rubric.
- AI-use disclosure expectations for your module.

## Steps

1. Build the claim-evidence matrix.
   - Use `templates/claim-evidence-matrix.md`.
   - Mark each claim as `SUPPORTED`, `PARTIAL`, `UNSUPPORTED`, `CONTRADICTED`, or `NOT_CHECKED`.

2. Remove weak claims.
   - Do not write from `UNSUPPORTED`, `CONTRADICTED`, or `NOT_CHECKED` claims.
   - Rewrite `PARTIAL` claims more narrowly.

3. Ask AI for structure.
   - Ask for an outline, paragraph logic, counterarguments, and missing evidence.
   - Do not ask AI to invent sources.

4. Draft with visible evidence.
   - Every paragraph should have a purpose and source trail.
   - Keep citations attached to checked sources.

5. Ask AI to critique.
   - Does the draft overclaim?
   - Are causal claims justified?
   - Are alternative explanations considered?
   - Are citations used accurately?

6. Final human review.
   - Open every cited source.
   - Check quotation, paraphrase, and citation format.
   - Complete `templates/ai-use-disclosure-log.md` if required.

## Output checklist

- [ ] The main argument is mine.
- [ ] Every citation points to a real source I opened.
- [ ] Every major claim has an evidence anchor.
- [ ] AI use is disclosed where required.
- [ ] Sensitive data was not uploaded.
- [ ] I can explain and defend the final work.
