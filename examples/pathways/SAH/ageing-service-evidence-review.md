# SAH Example: Ageing-Service Evidence Review

Audience: Smart Ageing and Health Management students.

## Example question

What evidence should managers consider before adopting a smart ageing service for community-based elder care?

## Safe source pack

Use:

- Public research papers.
- Public health or ageing-service guidelines.
- Public service-design reports.
- De-identified synthetic examples.

Do not upload:

- Patient records.
- Health identifiers.
- Care plans tied to real people.
- Clinical notes.
- Private family information.
- Data that could re-identify older adults or caregivers.

## Starter source manifest

| ID | Source | Source status | Why included | Human check |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SAH-S1 | Ageing-service review article | opened_full_text | Evidence on service models | Check outcome measures |
| SAH-S2 | Smart care technology report | opened_full_text | Implementation example | Check sponsor and bias |
| SAH-S3 | Public guideline | opened_full_text | Safety and governance boundary | Check jurisdiction |

## Claim-evidence examples

| Claim | Evidence needed | Support status |
|---|---|---|
| A smart ageing tool improves service coordination. | Study or service evaluation anchor. | NOT_CHECKED |
| Technology adoption improves health outcomes for all older adults. | Likely overclaim; needs strong evidence. | NOT_CHECKED |
| The tool can provide diagnosis or patient-specific advice. | Do not claim unless clinically authorized and sourced. | NOT_CHECKED |

## Human gate

Check:

- Whether a claim is managerial, clinical, or ethical.
- Whether the evidence comes from a comparable service context.
- Whether privacy, consent, and safeguarding boundaries are respected.
- Whether outcomes are wellbeing, satisfaction, efficiency, clinical outcome, or cost.

## Suggested output

A service evidence review:

- Service problem.
- Target users.
- Evidence base.
- Implementation requirements.
- Privacy and safety risks.
- Human accountability points.
- Evidence gaps.
