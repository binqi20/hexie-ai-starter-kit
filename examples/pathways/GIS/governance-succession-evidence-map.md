# GIS Example: Governance, Succession, and Digital Strategy Evidence Map

Audience: Enterprise Governance, Innovation, and Succession students.

## Example question

How can a family business design governance routines that support succession while adopting digital strategy?

## Safe source pack

Use public or university-authorized sources:

- Peer-reviewed articles on family business succession.
- Cases on governance and innovation.
- Public annual reports.
- Course readings.
- Public policy or industry reports.

Do not upload confidential firm documents or private family business records.

## Starter source manifest

| ID | Source | Source status | Why included | Human check |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| GIS-S1 | Succession paper | opened_full_text | Evidence on successor preparation | Check method and context |
| GIS-S2 | Governance case | opened_full_text | Governance routine example | Check case facts |
| GIS-S3 | Digital transformation report | metadata_only | Candidate background source | Do not cite until opened |

## Claim-evidence examples

| Claim | Evidence needed | Support status |
|---|---|---|
| Succession planning depends on both formal governance and informal trust. | Peer-reviewed source with family firm setting. | NOT_CHECKED |
| Digital strategy can intensify intergenerational tension. | Source with evidence on digital transformation and succession. | NOT_CHECKED |
| A board charter alone is enough to solve succession conflict. | Likely overclaim; needs evidence or removal. | NOT_CHECKED |

## Human gate

Check:

- Whether evidence comes from family firms, SMEs, listed firms, or another context.
- Whether claims about "success" are measured or interpreted.
- Whether digital strategy claims are based on evidence, not generic technology hype.

## Suggested output

A one-page evidence map with:

- Governance mechanism.
- Succession challenge.
- Digital strategy issue.
- Evidence anchor.
- Practical implication.
- What remains uncertain.
